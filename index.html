Frequently Asked Questions

How do freelancers receive a client booking?
When a consumer maps a request on our connected booking channels, the JMCY matching log algorithms cross-reference available therapists in the vicinity. Selected professionals instantly receive a push notification alert on their network web dashboard containing the date, schedule time, and targeted city area.
What is required to return to active status and receive new bookings after a session?
Upon fulfilling a massage treatment, log into your freelancer portal board and select the 'Mark Service as Completed' button. This closes out the current booking trail, instantly triggers your digital wallet checkout payout routing sequence, and automatically re-flags your system profile as 'Available' to receive fresh incoming local dispatches.
What are the core requirements to join the platform?
Applicants must present a valid government-issued ID, a recent clean NBI clearance record, and valid proof of massage therapy training or certification.
How does the payout mechanism distribute earnings?
Transactions are routed securely using enterprise payment gateways. Payout splits are executed automatically, delivering your base rate to your linked GCash or Maya wallet directly after client checkout validation.

Platform Operations & Commission Policy

Revenue Split Framework

By registering on the JMCY Freelance Network, independent contractors agree to our unified operational transaction framework:

  • Commission Parameters: JMCY retains a fixed platform operational fee percentage on each successful customer appointment booked through our matching systems (e.g., a standard 15% system fee split, leaving 85% gross income to the provider).
  • System Maintenances: This percentage covers technological infrastructure maintenance, secure escrow protection layer handling, local digital payment aggregator integrations (GCash/Maya processing charges), and centralized system administrative overhead.
  • Transparency Pledge: The complete monetary split breakdown showing your net earnings portion is displayed openly on your portal dashboard before you confirm and accept any incoming service request item.
